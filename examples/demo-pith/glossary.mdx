A short glossary of the words this handbook leans on. None are technical; all
are slippery enough that pinning them down is worth the page.

## Apparatus

The tools, references, conventions, and quiet routines that make a craft
repeatable. The apparatus is what you don't have to think about.

## Craft

The practice itself, distinct from the people who practise it or the
artefacts they make. The craft is bigger than any of us.

## Discipline

The willingness to do the small thing today because of who it makes you
tomorrow.

## Gathering

The first movement of a project. Reading, listening, sitting with. Easy to
mistake for procrastination; in fact load-bearing.

## Finishing

The last movement. Polish, proofreading, deliberate presentation. The part
that converts a draft into a gift.

## Maker

(aka practitioner)

Anyone whose work involves shaping something for someone else's use or
attention. Not narrowly an artist or engineer.

## Ornament

A flourish that does not earn its place. Distinct from a flourish that
*does*.

## Principle

A sentence I would defend in argument. A rule I would not.
