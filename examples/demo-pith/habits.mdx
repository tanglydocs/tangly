---
title: Habits
description: Small, stubborn, decisive.
---

Of all the topics in this handbook, habits are the ones I have least to say about and the most certainty around. Brief, then.

The work is paid for, in the long run, by a few small daily transactions: that you sit down, that you read for an hour, that you keep one notebook for everything. None of these will save a project on its own. All of them, together, change what kind of person makes the project.

There is a temptation to spend habit-energy on appearances: the perfect setup, the right pen, the correct app. Resist it. The thing that compounds is the act, not the staging.

If a habit becomes hard, ask whether you've made the action too large. The habit of *write for thirty minutes* will fail more days than it succeeds. The habit of *open the document* almost never fails. Pick the smallest action that puts you in front of the work; everything else takes care of itself, most of the time.
